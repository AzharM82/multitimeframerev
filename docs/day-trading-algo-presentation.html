The Core Engine

The Reversal Signal (Azhar_Reversal)

A two-layer ZigZag ported from a ThinkOrSwim study, validated penny-perfect against live TOS charts. It flags the exact bar where a swing turns.

How it detects a pivot

Layer 1 — find pivots: price source is Pivot = (H+L+C)/3, EMA-smoothed (period 5). A new pivot prints when price reverses by 0.5 × ATR(5). Pivots are placed retroactively on the true extreme bar.

Layer 2 — confirm direction: a bar-by-bar pass turns the signal positive once smoothed price closes back above the last low pivot.

The two flags

🟢
U1 — Bullish reversal
Signal crosses ≤0 → >0. Price has turned up. This is what we trade.
🔴
D1 — Bearish reversal
Signal crosses ≥0 → <0. Price has rolled over → exit / avoid.

Same engine runs in TypeScript (scanner) and Python (live broker) — both checked bar-for-bar against the original ThinkScript.

The Trade · 3 Rules

Entry, stop & target — defined by the chart

🟢

Entry = open of the bar after U1

Once the bullish reversal confirms, buy the open of the next bar. Market order — no fill window to miss.

🛑

Stop = low of the bar 2 before U1

The swing low that defined the reversal. If price breaks it, the thesis is wrong.

🎯

Target = entry × 1.05

A fixed +5% take-profit. Simple, repeatable, back-testable.

What I Built

Three surfaces, one engine

🖥️

MTF Reversal Scanner

The dashboard. AVWAP swings, a Bull List, a Day-Trades board and a Performance tab — the multi-timeframe confluence grid lives here. Emails the top setups daily.

🤖

StockAgentHub V3

The executor. Runs the Reversal engine server-side on Schwab candles every 3 min, places real MARKET orders, holds overnight, and journals every fill. Live with real money.

📷

Finviz + TOS scanner

The sourcing edge. Pulls a Finviz screener, loads each name on a real TOS chart, OCRs the actual reversal label, and posts only fresh up-reversals — no re-derivation drift.

All three share the same reversal math, so a signal means the same thing whether it shows on the board, fires an alert, or places an order.
